Modelling and Laser Cutting!



The Process:

The first issue I ran into on this project was that my cardboard was very inconsistent. I had bought sheets of cardboard from Amazon, and from the packaging warping the edges and the squishiness of the boards, each side of the cardboard came out different. I took measurements of all 4 sides and then averaged them to get an average thickness of 3.68mm.



After getting an average thickness of my cardboard, I created a test piece. I did cutouts from 3.5mm to 4.3mm. The larger sizes of 4.0 and bigger were very loose and didnâ€™t pass the shake test. The smallest 3 sizes were all acceptable sizes and passed the shake test, however I went with the smallest and tightest size so the finished project would really hold up under a firm shake. The pieces fit together nicely, but were snug that the structure would be sturdy.
